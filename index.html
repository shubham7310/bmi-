BMI Introduction

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a measure of body fatness based on height and weight that applies to adult men and women. BMI is calculated by dividing the body mass (in kilograms or pounds) by the square of the body height (in meters or feet). BMI is used to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems, such as underweight, overweight, obesity, etc. BMI is not intended to diagnose the body fatness or health of an individual, but it can provide a general indication of whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height45.

BMI is used to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems, such as underweight, overweight, obesity, etc. However, BMI is not a diagnostic tool and it may not reflect the actual body fatness or health status of an individual. Therefore, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before making any changes to your diet or physical activity based on your BMI.

BMI ranges and weight categories

The table below shows the BMI ranges and the weight categories for adults according to the World Health Organization (WHO).

Weight category BMI
Underweight <18.5
Normal weight 18.5–24.9
Overweight 25–29.9
Obesity BMI of 30 or greater
